Core Philosophy & Learning Principles

1. Comprehensible Input (i+1)

Definition: Expose learners to content slightly above their current level.

Implementation: - Reading Module: Texts auto-graded by known vocabulary % - Green: 98%+ known words (easy practice) - Yellow: 90-97% known (optimal learning zone) - Red: <90% known (frustration zone) - Listening Module: AI adjusts speech rate (0.7x â†’ 1.3x) - Vocabulary Module: New words appear in familiar sentence structures

2. Spaced Repetition (FSRS Algorithm)

Why not Ankiâ€™s SM-2?

Algorithm Release Strengths Weaknesses
SM-2 (Anki) 1988 Simple, proven Fixed formula, no personalization

Theory (Swain, 1985): Producing language (speaking/writing) helps learners: - Notice gaps in knowledge - Test hypotheses about grammar - Develop fluency (automaticity)

4. Shadowing: The Ultimate Pronunciation Tool

What is Shadowing?

Listen to audio (speech, podcast, dialogue) and simultaneously repeat what you hearâ€”matching: - Pronunciation - Intonation - Rhythm - Emotion

Cognitive Benefits: 1. Phonological loop training â€” improves working memory 2. Prosody acquisition â€” natural speech patterns 3. Automaticity â€” reduces cognitive load over time

Implementation:

User flow:
1. Select audio (30s-2min clip)
2. Listen 1x without speaking (comprehension check)
3. Shadow 3x:
   - First: Focus on pronunciation
   - Second: Match rhythm/intonation
   - Third: Add emotion/naturalness
4. Record yourself (optional)
5. AI compares your audio to native (pronunciation score)

Content Library: - Movie/TV dialogues (Friends, Breaking Bad, etc.) - TED Talk snippets - Podcast clips (news, storytelling) - Shadowing-specific lessons (minimal music, clear speech)

5. Desirable Difficulties (Bjork)

Principle: Learning should feel challenging, not easy.

What We Avoid: - âŒ Multiple choice (too easy, encourages guessing) - âŒ Translation crutches (prevents direct L2 thinking) - âŒ Immediate hints (robs opportunity to struggle)
